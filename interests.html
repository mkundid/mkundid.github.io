Currently Reading

Zero to One

Author: Peter Thiel

Review (In work)

Peter Thiel is a well known entrepreneur who kicked off his rise to fame by founding PayPal and is more recently known as an investor in many highly successful companies in the technology industry. In Zero to One, he discusses his idea of start-ups and their ability to change the world by making significant leaps in technology. While I'm only 12% into the book, it's clear that his ideas are ambitions and concepts often go against the grain of public opinion. Early in the book, or as far as I've gotten, he discusses the positive impacts monopolies can have on the overall progress of society. While I disagree on the surface, he brings up some great points about price-wars in competitive industries that become more important than technological growth. Instead, start-ups should focus on creating something new and gain a monopoly in that specific product/technology. Someone new will come along later with a monopoly in a better technology. Since I'm only a few pages into the book, I'm rambling right now...but a real review will come soon.

MOVIES

Last Watched

Interstellar

Review

In my opinion, The Martian was a much better movie than Interstellar since, while sometimes stretched, the science used and described in the movie was much more realistic. Interstellar is one of those movies that starts off strong, but quickly overcomplicates the scientific concepts and requires your intense focus. A movie shouldn't require this much focus to maintain your understanding of the plot. After stepping away for a few minutes to grab coffee, I realized I missed a key plot line and I instantly felt like I was watching Inception while half-asleep. However, the acting and special effects made Interstellar slightly more enjoyable to watch.
